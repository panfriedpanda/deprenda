Trust & Authenticity

Provenance &
Authentication

Buying a rare object requires trust. Here is exactly how we establish it — and what we provide with every piece.

Why It Matters

The Value
of Knowing

Rimowa has become a genuinely collectible brand. Limited edition pieces, airline collaborations, and rare colorways command significant premiums on the secondary market — and that means the secondary market also has fakes, misrepresented pieces, and stories that don't hold up to scrutiny.

The de Prenda Collection is in a position that cannot be replicated. These pieces did not come from a reseller, an auction house, or a third-party vendor. They came directly from a former owner of the company itself — held in a family home in Belgium for decades.

That is not a claim we ask you to accept on faith. It is something we can document, demonstrate, and show you in person. The burden of proof is on us, and we welcome it.

What Every Purchase Includes
  • Certificate of provenance signed by the de Prenda family
  • Documentation of original acquisition where available
  • Detailed condition report for the specific piece
  • Series identification and historical context notes
  • Photographs of the piece at Château de Prenda
  • Direct contact with the family post-purchase
  • Option of in-person verification before purchase
The Foundation

Three Pillars
of Trust

I
Direct Ownership

There is no more direct provenance possible than a former owner's personal collection. These pieces did not pass through auction houses or resellers. They went from the brand to the owner, and from the owner to you. The chain has two links.

II
Physical Verification

We actively encourage in-person viewings at the château before any significant purchase. You may inspect every piece, examine hardware, review documents, and ask every question you have. Nothing is restricted. Nothing is staged.

III
Written Documentation

Every piece is accompanied by a certificate of provenance and, where available, supporting documentation. Our ongoing relationship with each buyer is part of the provenance — we remain reachable long after the sale.

Common Questions

Frequently
Asked

How do I know these are genuinely from the former owner? +
We provide documentation, we are available for in-person meetings, and we encourage you to visit the château where the collection has been kept. The physical environment and the family's direct involvement are the strongest form of verification available.
Are these the original, unmodified pieces? +
Yes. Every piece is in its original condition as acquired. We do not restore, refurbish, or modify any piece before sale. Condition is documented honestly and transparently in each listing.
What documentation comes with each piece? +
Each piece comes with a certificate of provenance, a detailed condition report, and historical series notes. Where original acquisition documentation exists, it is included. We also provide photographs of the piece at the château.
Can I visit the château before purchasing? +
Absolutely — in fact, we encourage it for high-value pieces. Private viewings are available by appointment and are complimentary. There is no obligation to purchase. Simply contact us to arrange a visit.
Do you ship internationally? +
Yes. We work with specialist couriers experienced in handling high-value collectibles. Full insurance is arranged for every shipment. We always prefer an in-person viewing first, but we understand that is not always possible.
Is this an official Rimowa venture? +
No. The de Prenda Collection is a private family sale of pieces from the former owner's personal collection. It has no official affiliation with Rimowa GmbH or LVMH. Rimowa is a registered trademark of its respective owners.
